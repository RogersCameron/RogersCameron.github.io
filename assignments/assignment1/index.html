Field Lacrosse

Lacrosse is a fast-paced and dynamic team sport that originated among Native American tribes and has evolved into a popular game played internationally. The game is played with a small rubber ball and long-handled sticks with a net at the end, known as lacrosse sticks. The objective is to score goals by shooting the ball into the opposing team's goal, which is protected by a goalkeeper. Lacrosse is known for its high-scoring nature and requires a combination of speed, agility, and strategic teamwork.

Positions in Field Lacrosse

  • Attack

    • Attackmen (Attack): Positioned near the opponent's goal, their main responsibility is to score goals.

  • Midfield

    • Midfielders (Middies): Cover the entire field and play both offense and defense. They are involved in transition play.

  • Defense

    • Defensemen (Defense): Positioned near their own goal, they aim to prevent the opposing team from scoring.

  • Goalie

    • Goaltender (Goalie): Guards the goal and tries to block the opponent's shots.

More on field lacrosse

Box lacrosse, played indoors with six players per team, is a fast-paced and physically intense variant of traditional field lacrosse. The confined space of the arena encourages quick decision-making, close-quarters skills, and precise stick work. Utilizing the boards for checks and strategic moves, players engage in a dynamic and high-energy game. Overall, box lacrosse provides a thrilling and compact experience for both players and spectators.
